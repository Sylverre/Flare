Overview of a Pilot Deployment

Before installing hardware throughout your environment, begin with a pilot deployment. A pilot is typically a single-site test performed on a representative sample region within your data center. This small deployment provides the opportunity to test functionality, gain experience, and pinpoint any areas of concern prior to the full roll-out.

A pilot deployment can be approached in stages:

  1. Assess your environment (or review the assessment done prior to purchase).
  2. Install for a pilot deployment.
  3. Configure IR Locators and readers.
  4. Deploy readers, tags, and IR Locators.
  5. Add assets to pilot installation of to prove data collection.

  6. Assess pilot performance, and adjust plans for roll-out accordingly.

A full roll-out of is quite similar in terms of hardware, but differs in software installation and configuration. For a full roll-out, a database must be created, additional Zone Managers may be required depending on deployment configuration, assets must be tagged, and information loaded into the system. System Administration tasks such as User Access setting are not typically addressed during a pilot, but must be completed before is made available to end users.
